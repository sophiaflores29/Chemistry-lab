Introduction and hypothesis

PH Scale

Introduction

In this experiment, we explored the pH levels of various everyday products to determine

whether they are acidic, neutral, or basic. The pH scale, which ranges from 0 to 14, helps

above 7 indicate basicity. For our investigation, we tested a variety of common items including yogurt,

Lipton tea, milk, orange juice, alcohol, lemon, chloride, bicarbonate, pineapple juice, Ronald pie,

and Nelson pie.By measuring their pH levels, we aimed to identify where each product falls on the pH scale and better

understand their chemical properties. This experiment not only enhances our knowledge of pH in real-world

contexts, but also reinforces how chemistry applies to the products we use and consume every day.

Hypothesis

If we test the pH levels of everyday products,

then acidic items like lemon and orange juice will have pH values below 7,

neutral items like milk will have a pH around 7, and basic items like bicarbonate will

have pH values above 7, because their chemical composition determines their

placement on the pH scale.
